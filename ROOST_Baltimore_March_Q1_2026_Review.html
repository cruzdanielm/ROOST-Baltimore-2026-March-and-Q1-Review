Executive Summary

The asset is tracking materially ahead of plan — led by demand, not rate.

March delivered a breakout operating month. Gross Rental Revenue of $431K came in 19% ahead of budget and 34% above prior year, producing Net Operating Income of $84K against a $40K plan. Quarter-to-date, the asset has swung from a $(11)K Q1 2025 loss to +$73K NOI in 2026 — running 88% ahead of budget on essentially flat operating expenses. Q1 2026 represents the first positive first quarter in the asset's history.

The one-line read

A strong first quarter driven by short-stay demand and disciplined expense management — with three strategic areas of focus: (1) ADR is trailing the Baltimore submarket (ARI 94.7 in March), (2) 30+ night extended-stay revenue has declined ~$103K YTD as legacy contracts rolled off, and (3) F&B is tracking behind budget on COGS discipline. Each is addressable in Q2 and represents the next layer of upside to asset returns.

What drove the quarter

Performance highlights

  • Transient demand lift. Short-stay rental revenue up $116K (+47%) YoY in March. The 3–6 night segment alone contributed $111K of incremental revenue YTD.
  • Market-leading occupancy. March occupancy of 74.0% vs comp set 68.1% — MPI of 108.7, the strongest monthly share reading of the cycle.
  • Expense discipline. Q1 total operating expenses of $905K landed dollar-flat against a $905K budget, despite higher volume — evidence of genuine operating leverage.
  • Favorable line items: Housekeeping supplies $10K under budget YTD, marketing $5K under, apartment expenses $10K under.

Strategic areas of focus

Three items to manage into Q2

Rate positioning. Comp-set ADR outpaced us by ~$11 in March and $18 at the submarket class — an ARI of 94.7 indicates rate headroom to monetize into the peak-season demand window.

Extended-stay replacement. 30+ night revenue is down $103K YTD as Synergy Marine, Atlantis UGS, and other legacy anchors scaled down. Rebuilding the long-stay pipeline protects the occupancy floor.

F&B cost structure. Combined Q1 food cost is running 57% of food sales vs 30% budget; liquor sales $40K under plan YTD. Gross-profit recapture at Vessel is the Q2 focus.

What moved the month

Three drivers behind the $45K beat

1 · Transient volume. The ROOST short-stay book ran 33% above plan as 1–6 night stays expanded faster than the revenue model assumed.

2 · Flow-through efficiency. Approximately 65% of incremental revenue dropped to NOI — volume arrived without proportional step-ups in labor or supplies.

3 · Mix pressure, absorbed. Terrapin multifamily revenue was $24K under plan as units transition out of long-lease inventory into short-stay — a deliberate portfolio rotation delivering higher nightly yield at some cost to stabilized base.

Housekeeping Performance · March

Efficient service model holding as volume scales

Total housekeeping cost (supplies + labor allocation + contract support) tracked at $17.6K in March on apartment supplies, plus allocated housekeeping labor embedded in departmental compensation. YTD, apartment/housekeeping supplies are running 19.5% under budget — $10K of genuine savings despite higher volume.

Service structure. The department currently operates on a hybrid model combining in-house rooms labor with flex capacity for peak turnover days. This has protected guest-experience consistency while short-stay volume scaled +22% YoY — without requiring a proportional headcount expansion.

Cost implication. Housekeeping cost per occupied room night is running within industry tolerance for upper-upscale extended-stay product. As short-stay volume continues to rise through peak season, the Q2 focus is evaluating whether flex capacity should be converted to fixed in-house hires to capture further cost efficiency at scale.

NOI Per Available Unit · Interpretation

Positive indicator relative to stabilization benchmarks

March NOI / unit = $84,347 ÷ 121 units = $697 per unit. Annualized at this March run-rate, that equates to ~$8,400 NOI per unit per year.

Q1 NOI / unit = $72,616 ÷ 121 units = $600 per unit YTD, or an annualized pace of ~$2,400 per unit.

Interpretation. March's run-rate is consistent with a mid-cycle stabilized extended-stay asset in a secondary urban market. Q1 as a whole still lags what a fully stabilized comparable would produce (typical stabilized upper-upscale NOI/unit in the $10–14K range), reflecting (a) Q1 seasonality, (b) the ongoing mix rotation from Terrapin multifamily to ROOST short-stay, and (c) F&B NOP drag while Vessel ramps.

Health signal. The trajectory is the read. March alone is tracking at ~3x the Q1 pace, and April MTD demand signals (MPI 126) reinforce the trend. The underlying unit economics are healthy and improving; the asset is on a credible path to stabilized NOI productivity by year-end.

Revenue & Expense Drivers

What actually moved March & Q1
Demand · Rate · Mix · Cost
Drivers · March & Q1 YTD

A demand story on the top line; a discipline story on the P&L.

Decomposing the revenue beat: approximately 80% of the $42K Q1 revenue beat vs budget came from occupancy outperformance (primarily in March), and ~20% from ancillary and cancellation fees. On the expense side, every departmental and undistributed category except Utilities and Other Operating landed at or under plan.

Revenue drivers

Where the top-line beat originated

  • March occupancy lift. 74.0% vs submarket 66.1% and comp 68.1%. ~15 pts YoY occupancy growth.
  • Rate repositioning. ADR +19.1% YoY, though still $11 below comp. Higher-rate transient replacing lower-rate extended-stay.
  • Mix shift to short-stay. The 1–6 night segment drove $230K of incremental revenue YoY.
  • Direct channel build. Roost Baltimore Direct Sales ($12K) and Booking.com ($35K combined) now contributing in 2026.
  • Group pickup. GR CORP and GR SMERF segments added $75K YTD (vs $5K PY) — early signals of a building group/meetings pipeline.
  • Event-driven pockets. Baltimore Orioles season added $15K vs PY; leisure pickup around MLB Opening Week.

Expense drivers

What held the cost base in line

  • Housekeeping supplies. $10K under plan YTD on linen rotation and bath-amenity optimization.
  • Marketing discipline. Advertising $47K vs $56K plan — demand arrived without full spend.
  • Compensation on budget. Total comp $313K vs $316K plan despite higher volume — real labor leverage emerging.
  • Utility overrun. +$8.5K YTD (Electric +$7K, Internet +$5K). Cold-weather and vendor contract drivers.
  • Housekeeping support labor. $62K YTD of contracted support capacity, used to flex with demand — structural review planned for Q2.
  • Commissions. +$9K YTD vs plan on transient mix (higher OTA and direct booking fees).
Bottom line

Volume is doing the work. Every dollar of incremental revenue is flowing through at high margin because the expense base is structurally fixed to a 121-unit building — producing genuine operating leverage as occupancy rises. The counterbalance is asymmetry: if demand softens, Utilities, Housekeeping support, and Commissions don't flex down proportionally. The Q2 plan builds explicit downside hedges for each.

Risks & Opportunities · Q2 Outlook

Four risks to manage and five opportunities to monetize.

The asset is tracking well. Three structural risks — rate gap, extended-stay replacement, and F&B cost structure — could compress the flow-through earned in Q1 if unaddressed. Peak-season demand signals are strong and should be priced into rate and sales strategy now.

Risk · Rate Compression

ARI 94.7 in March (and 101.9 YTD) indicates trailing comp-set pricing. Every $5 of ADR recaptured equals ~$120K annualized at current occupancy.

Risk · Extended-Stay Pipeline

30+ night revenue down $103K YTD as Synergy Marine, Atlantis UGS, AMN, and Sirva scaled down. Replacement accounts are needed before summer seasonality.

Risk · F&B COGS

Food cost 57% of food sales YTD (vs 30% budget). Vessel beverage mix $40K under plan YTD. Gross-profit recapture is the Q2 F&B agenda.

Risk · Guest Rating Drift

Avg rating 4.38 Q1 (below 4.60 target); NPS -6 pts YoY. Promoter share slipping ahead of peak-season review volume.

Opp · Q2 Demand Momentum

April MTD MPI 126.4 — the highest of the cycle. Price into it. Closing the ARI gap on compression nights is the highest-value move of Q2.

Opp · Group / SMERF Segment

$75K YTD vs $5K PY and growing. Orioles, Loyola, Albion, Alpha Phi Alpha are early wins — a dedicated group segment with defined rate ladder builds from here.

Opp · Direct Channel

Roost Baltimore Direct at $12K YTD; Roost Friends & Family pilot traction. Direct-channel bookings carry 15%+ better bottom-line margin vs OTA.

Opp · Vessel Ramp

First quarter of operation. Liquor program reset, happy-hour activation, and Little Wing cross-pollination can meaningfully close the $30K YTD NOP gap.

Opp · Johns Hopkins Expansion

Patient Program now $11K YTD from zero. Monthly, Weekly, and Daily Rate products all contributing. An institutional relationship to deepen systematically.

Strategic summary

Q1 outperformance is real, and protecting it requires action. It rests on three pillars: (a) transient demand strength, (b) favorable cost absorption, and (c) a deferred replacement cycle on extended-stay accounts. Securing all three is the substance of the Q2 action plan — most urgently on rate discipline, extended-stay sales pipeline, and F&B cost structure.
